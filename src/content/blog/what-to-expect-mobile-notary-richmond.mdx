Booking a mobile notary shouldn't feel mysterious. Here is exactly what happens, step by step, when you book with me.

## 1. You reach out

Call, text, or submit the contact form. Tell me the service (single signature, loan signing, apostille, estate document, etc.), the location, and roughly when you want it done. If you are not sure which service you need, just describe the situation and I will tell you straight.

## 2. I confirm availability

I reply within the hour, usually much less. I will confirm the exact time, the full price (no surprise fees), the address, and what you need to have ready: valid photo ID, the document itself if you have it, and any witnesses you want present. If a witness is required and you do not have one, I will coordinate that.

## 3. Before I arrive

I print, assemble, and verify the documents. For loan signings, I review the package in advance so nothing is missed. For apostilles, I confirm whether you need a state or federal authentication, or both. For estate signings, I confirm the witness arrangement and any facility visiting rules.

## 4. The signing itself

I arrive on time with everything I need. I verify your identification, direct you through the document page by page, and show you exactly where to sign, initial, and date. I do not interpret the document or give legal advice, that is not the notary's role in Virginia, but I make sure the signing is complete and correct.

## 5. After the signing

I leave you with clean copies of everything. For loan signings, I coordinate return shipping with the title company. For apostilles, I submit the paperwork and update you as status changes. For everything else, you are done.

## How long does all this take?

Single-signature notarizations: 10–20 minutes on-site. Loan signings: 45–75 minutes. Estate packages: usually 30–60 minutes, longer if the signer wants to take their time (which is absolutely fine).
