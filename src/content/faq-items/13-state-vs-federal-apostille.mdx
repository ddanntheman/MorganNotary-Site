question: What's the difference between state and federal apostille?
answer: >-
  A state apostille is issued by the Virginia Secretary of State and
  authenticates documents issued by Virginia authorities, birth
  certificates, marriage certificates, diplomas from Virginia schools,
  Virginia-notarized documents, and so on. A federal apostille is
  issued by the U.S. Department of State and authenticates documents
  issued by federal agencies (FBI background checks, IRS documents,
  etc.). Some countries require both. Send me what you have and I'll
  tell you which you need.
