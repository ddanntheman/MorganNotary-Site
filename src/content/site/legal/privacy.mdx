**Last updated:** April 16, 2026

Morgan Danner, Notary LLC ("we," "our," or "us") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information about you when you use this website or book a service.

## Information we collect

- **Contact form submissions:** name, email, phone, and the details you provide about your notary request.
- **Analytics:** we use [Plausible Analytics](https://plausible.io/), a privacy-focused analytics service that does not use cookies and does not track individuals. Aggregate metrics such as page views, referrers, and rough geographic region (country/state) are collected.
- **Business records:** for signings we complete, Virginia law requires us to maintain a journal of notarial acts. This journal contains your name, the date and type of act, and the type of identification shown, not the content of the document.

## How we use information

- To confirm, schedule, and perform the service you requested.
- To communicate with you about your signing (reminders, confirmations, follow-ups).
- To comply with Virginia notary recordkeeping requirements.
- To operate and improve this website.

We **do not** sell, rent, or trade your information. We **do not** share your information with marketers.

## Third parties

- **Formspree**, processes contact-form submissions on our behalf. See [Formspree's privacy policy](https://formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy).
- **Square, Inc.**, processes appointment bookings and payments. See [Square's privacy policy](https://squareup.com/us/en/legal/general/privacy).
- **Vercel, Inc.**, hosts this website. See [Vercel's privacy policy](https://vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy).

## Your rights

Virginia residents have rights under the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA), including the right to know what personal data we hold and the right to request deletion of information that is not required by law to be retained. To exercise these rights, email morgan@morgannotary.com.

## Security

We use industry-standard security practices (HTTPS, encrypted storage, access controls). Notary journals are stored securely and retained for the period required by Virginia law.

## Changes

We will update this policy as our practices change. The "Last updated" date at the top will always reflect the most recent revision.
