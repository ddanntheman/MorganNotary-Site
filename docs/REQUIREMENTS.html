Hard constraints
UPL (Unauthorized Practice of Law) is a legal hard line. Virginia Code § 54.1-3900 et seq. — a non-lawyer notary cannot explain document contents, interpret legal terms, or advise on whether terms are favorable. Every piece of copy referencing what Morgan does with a document MUST say "direct you through" or "walk you through where to sign" — NEVER "explain" or "interpret" or "advise." The agent MUST flag any copy that violates this and fix it before rendering.
Virginia statutory notary fee max: $10 per notarial act. All package pricing must be internally auditable — travel fee + single notarial act ≤ $10 for the notarization component. Package descriptions MUST specify what portion is "travel / printing / convenience" vs "notarial act" to defend the pricing model if challenged. Drew is responsible for confirming this math with a VA attorney before launch; agent MUST include a footnote on the pricing page noting "Notarial acts billed at the Virginia statutory rate of $10 per act. Package prices reflect travel, printing, witness coordination, and scheduling convenience."
No real client PII before explicit approval. The 5 testimonials in the brand guide are composites — they MUST be labeled internally as such and swapped only when real, written-consent reviews are collected. Do not invent client names. Do not auto-generate case studies.
No tracking cookies without consent banner. Plausible is cookieless and can ship without a banner. If GA4 is added (SHOULD, not MUST), the site MUST include a cookie consent banner compliant with VCDPA (Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act). See Section 50.
